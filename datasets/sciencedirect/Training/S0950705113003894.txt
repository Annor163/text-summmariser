@&#MAIN-TITLE@&#
2-Dimension uncertain linguistic power generalized weighted aggregation operator and its application in multiple attribute group decision making

@&#HIGHLIGHTS@&#
We proposed 2DULPGA operator and 2DULPGWA operator.We investigated some desirable properties of the 2DULPGA and2DULPGWA operators.We studied some special cases of the generalized parameters in these operators.A group decision making method based these operators were proposed.

@&#KEYPHRASES@&#
Linguistic variables,Power average (PA),Power generalized aggregation operators,2-Dimension uncertain linguistic information,Multiple attribute decision making,

@&#ABSTRACT@&#
The 2-dimension uncertain linguistic variables add a subjective evaluation on the reliability of the evaluation results given by decision makers, so they can better express fuzzy information. At the same time, the power average (PA) operator has the characteristic of capturing the correlations of the aggregated arguments. In this paper, we propose some power aggregation operators, including 2-dimension uncertain linguistic power generalized aggregation operator (2DULPGA) and 2-dimension uncertain linguistic power generalized weighted aggregation operator (2DULPGWA), and discuss some properties and special cases of them. Finally, with respect to the multiple attribute group decision making problems in which the attribute values take the form of the 2-dimension uncertain linguistic information, the method based on some power generalized aggregation operators is proposed, and two examples are given to verify the developed approach and to demonstrate its effectiveness.

@&#INTRODUCTION@&#
Multiple attribute decision making (MADM) refers to ranking the limited alternatives or selecting the best one from them by utilizing the known information. Since Churchman et al. [1] introduced the multi-attribute decision making in 1957 and applied it to the decision of selecting the enterprise investment policy, the theory and methods of the classical MADM have been perfect. However, owing to the complexity and uncertainty of objective things and the fuzziness of human thinking, a large number of MADM problems are uncertain, which are called the uncertain multiple attribute decision making (UMADM) problems. Among these decision making problems, it is hard or expensive to measure the attribute values of the alternatives by real numbers, while it is easy to express the their evaluation values by linguistic information. Zedeh [2] firstly introduced the definition of the linguistic variable and applied it to the fuzzy reasoning, which is the foundation of the decision making based on the linguistic variable. Recently, Zhu et al. [3] proposed the 2-dimension linguistic evaluation information to process such kind of linguistic fuzzy decision problems, such as communication review of the science and technology project or awards, blind review of journal papers, and so on. That is to say, decision makers can use a two-dimensional linguistic variable to describe their evaluation to an object. One is used to describe linguistic evaluation result for an attribute given by decision maker; the other is used to describe the subjective evaluation of decision makers on the reliability of their given results. Obviously, 2-dimension linguistic variable can more accurately reflect the evaluation of decision makers on objects.At present, research on multi-attribute group decision making based on 2-dimension linguistic information is less, and the main research achievements are shown as follows. Zhu et al. [3] proposed the concept of 2-dimension linguistic information, and used the subjective evidence reasoning theory to quantify the 2-dimension linguistic information, then proposed a decision method by improving evidence synthesis rules based on the conflict analysis. Liu and Zhang [4] further extended 2-dimension linguistic information to 2-dimension uncertain linguistic information, and developed a method to deal with the multiple attribute group decision making problems in which the attribute weight is unknown and attribute values take the form of 2-dimension uncertain linguistic information. The method of process the second dimensional uncertain linguistic information is that it is converted to the expert’s subjective weight, finally the TOPSIS method is used to rank the alternatives. Yu et al. [5] firstly converted the 2-dimension linguistic information to the generalized triangle fuzzy number, and then developed 2-dimension linguistic weighted average (2DLWA) operator and 2-dimension linguistic ordered weighted average (2DLOWA) operator, finally, proposed the ranking method based on these operators. Zhang et al. [6] established 2-tuple linguistic evidential reasoning theory, then built the II dimension identification framework reflecting the knowledge and behavior of decision makers, and proposed 2-tuple linguistic evidential reasoning method. It is clear that these researches above get rid of the some basic issues, such as the operational rules of 2-dimension linguistic information, and so on.The information aggregation operators are an interesting research topic, which are receiving increasing concern [7–23]. The power-average (PA) operator has the characteristic of capturing the correlations of the aggregated arguments, so since Yager [15] firstly proposed a power-average (PA) operator and a power OWA (POWA) operator, the research on PA operator and its applications is becoming a focus. Xu and Yager [16] developed the power-geometric (PG) operator, power-ordered-geometric (POG) operator, power-ordered-weighted-geometric (POWG) operator, uncertain PG (UPG) operator, and uncertain power-ordered-weighted-geometric (UPOWG) operator, and studied some properties of these operators. Then, the approaches to group decision making are developed based on these operators. Xu and Wang [17] developed some linguistic aggregation operators, including 2-tuple linguistic power average (2TLPA) operator, 2-tuple linguistic weighted power average (2TLWPA) operator, and 2-tuple linguistic power-ordered-weighted average (2TLPOWA) operator, then studied some properties of these operators, such as idempotency and boundary. Then, two methods were proposed to deal with group decision making problems under linguistic information. Zhang [18] proposed a wide range of hesitant fuzzy power aggregation operators for hesitant fuzzy information, and discussed several useful properties of the operators and the relationships between them. Then the new aggregation operators are utilized to develop techniques for multiple attribute group decision making with hesitant fuzzy information, and some decision methods were proposed. Xu [19] developed a series of power aggregation operators for aggregating the intuitionistic fuzzy numbers, and studied various properties of these operators, and then some approaches to multiple attribute group decision making with intuitionistic fuzzy information and interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy information were proposed.Because the 2-dimension linguistic variable can more accurately reflect the evaluation of decision makers on objects, and PA operator can capture the correlations of the aggregated arguments. Combining the 2-dimension linguistic variables and PA operator, This paper proposes some power aggregation operators, including 2-dimension uncertain linguistic power generalized aggregation operator (2DULPGA), and 2-dimension uncertain linguistic power generalized weighted aggregation operator (2DULPGWA), and some properties and special cases of them are discussed. Finally, the decision making method is developed based on these operators to solve the multiple attribute group decision making problems with 2-dimension uncertain linguistic information, and two examples are given to compare the proposed method with the existing ones.The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. In Section 1, we give an introduction of the research background. Section 2 briefly reviews some basic concepts and operational rules related to the uncertain linguistic variables, and some PA operators. In Section 3, we give an introduction of 2-dimension uncertain linguistic variables. In Section 4, we develop some power aggregation operators based on2-dimension uncertain linguistic variables, and some properties and special cases of them are discussed. In Section 5, we apply the these operators to the multiple attribute group problems in which the attributes take the form of 2-dimension uncertain linguistic variables, and give the decision making steps. In Section 6, gives two examples to illustrate the decision making steps based on the proposed method.
