@&#MAIN-TITLE@&#
Information granulation and uncertainty measures in interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy information systems

@&#HIGHLIGHTS@&#

@&#KEYPHRASES@&#

@&#ABSTRACT@&#
Information granulation and entropy are main approaches for investigating the uncertainty of information systems, which have been widely employed in many practical domains. In this paper, information granulation and uncertainty measures for interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy binary granular structures are addressed. First, we propose the representation of interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy information granules and examine some operations of interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy granular structures. Second, the interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy information granularity is introduced to depict the distinguishment ability of an interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy granular structure (IIFGS), which is a natural extension of fuzzy information granularity. Third, we discuss how to scale the uncertainty of an IIFGS using the extended information entropy and the uncertainty among interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy granular structures using the expanded mutual information derived from the presented intuitionistic fuzzy information entropy. Fourth, we discovery the relationship between the developed interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy information entropy and the intuitionistic fuzzy information granularity presented in this paper.

@&#INTRODUCTION@&#
Granular Computing has emerged as a unified and coherent platform of constructing, describing, and processing information granules (Zadeh, 1998; Pedrycz, 2012). In GrC, a granule is a set of objects integrated together with the equivalence, indistinguishability, similarity, and proximity of functionality (Pedrycz, 2002; Lin, 2009). The set of granules provides a representation of the unit with respect to a particular level of granularity. The process of constructing information granules is called information granulation, which granulates a universe of discourse into a family of disjoint or overlapping granules. The purpose of GrC is to find an approximation scheme which can effectively solve a complex problem at a certain level of granulation. Since the concept has been proposed, GrC has become a fast growing field of research in recent years (Pedrycz, 2011; Wu and Leung, 2011).The granulation of objects induced by an equivalent relation is a set of equivalence classes, where each equivalence class can be regarded as an equivalent information granule (Pawlak and Skowron, 2007). Similarly, all tolerance classes induced by a tolerance relation can be seen as the tolerance information granules (Qian et al., 2010). Generally speaking, on the basis of a binary relation (equivalence, similarity, tolerance, dominance, covering-based, neighborhood-based, etc.), one can easily granulate all objects in a crisp information system into a set of information granules, called a binary granular structure (Abo-Tabl, 2011; Dai et al., 2012; Hu et al., 2010; Qian et al., 2011; Skowron et al., 2012). Furthermore, by using a fuzzy binary relation, objects can be granulated into a set of fuzzy information granules, called a fuzzy binary granular structure (Hu et al., 2006, 2007, 2008; Ganivada et al., 2011).In GrC, an important concept is information granularity of a granular structure, which is a measure of uncertainty about its actual structure. In general, the information granularity represents discernibility ability of information in a granular structure. The smaller the information granularity, the stronger its discernibility ability. How to calculate the information granularity of a granular structure has always been an important issue. Nowadays, several forms of information granularity have been presented according to various views and targets (Qian et al., 2011).Information entropy and information granularity are two main approaches to measure the uncertainty of a granular structure (Liang and Qian, 2008; Pedrycz, 2011). The entropy of a granular structure, as defined by Shannon (Shannon, 1948), gives a measure of the uncertainty about its actual structure. Because Shannon entropy is defined by the probability of each equivalence class, it cannot be used to measure the uncertainty of a fuzzy granular structure. To overcome this drawback, Hu et al. (2010) extended Shannon entropy to fuzzy-binary granular structures and used this variant to characterize the uncertainty of fuzzy rough sets and fuzzy probability rough sets.Qian et al. (2011) studied fuzzy binary granular structures’ operators and partial order relations, measures for fuzzy information granularity, and axiomatic approach to binary fuzzy information granularity and fuzzy information entropy. Another important mathematical structure that can represent imperfect and/or imprecise information is the intuitionistic fuzzy set (IFS) developed by Atanassov (1986). The IFS is considered a natural extension of Zadeh’s fuzzy sets, and is defined by a pair of membership functions: a fuzzy set provides only the a degree to which an element belongs to a universe, whereas an IFS yields both a membership and a non-membership degree. The membership and non-membership values generate an indeterminacy index that models the hesitancy in deciding the degree to which an object satisfies a particular property. Recently, IFS theory has been successfully applied in decision analysis and pattern recognition (Grzegorzewski, 2011; Wu and Zhang, 2011). In IFS theory, two closed intervals are used so that the subsets of [0,1] can indicate membership and non-membership degrees; Atanassov and Gargov (1989) presented the interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy set (IIFS). Can the granulation of objects, partial order relations, uncertainty measures for information granularity in fuzzy information systems be generalized to interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy systems? In this paper, we try to answer this question.The remainder of the paper is organized as follows. Section 2 briefly introduces the preliminary issues considered in the study, such as the notions of fuzzy binary granular structures and their basic properties, IIFSs and their operations, and then proposes an important concept: cardinality of IIFSs. Section 3 presents interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy binary relation, operators of interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy binary granular structures and their three partial-order relations. In Section 4, we propose interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy information granularity and rough entropy to measure interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy binary granular structures. In Section 5, we extend Shannon information entropy and information entropy to measure information content of an interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy binary granular structure, and develop joint entropies, conditional entropies, and mutual information to measure information variation between two interval-valued intuitionistic fuzzy binary granular structures.
